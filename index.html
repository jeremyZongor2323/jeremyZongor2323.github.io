Poverty in the World

Understanding the challenge, its causes, and how we can make a difference.

About Poverty

Poverty is not just about not having enough money. It is a multidimensional issue that concerns a lack of education, health, and living standards. The World Bank defines extreme poverty as living on less than $2.15 a day.

It means facing daily challenges to access basic needs such as food, clean water, and shelter. Understanding poverty is the first step toward solving it.

Causes of Poverty

Poverty is caused by a complex mix of factors. Some of the primary causes include:

  • Lack of Education: Without access to education, people cannot develop the skills needed to find better jobs.
  • Conflict and Instability: War and violence destroy infrastructure and force people to flee their homes.
  • Limited Access to Healthcare: Poor health can prevent people from working and drains family resources.
  • Climate Change: Natural disasters and changing weather patterns affect agriculture, a primary source of income for many.

Effects of Poverty

The consequences of poverty are severe and far-reaching:

  • Malnutrition & Hunger: Families cannot afford nutritious food.
  • Child Labor: Children are often forced to work instead of going to school.
  • Social Exclusion: People in poverty often lack a voice in their communities.
  • Reduced Life Expectancy: Lack of healthcare and poor living conditions lead to shorter lives.
